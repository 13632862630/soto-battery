Huawei VR Recruit: Former Oculus Chief Researcher Will Serve As Chief Scientist 

December 30, 2016

       Steve Lavalle, a former Oculus(well-known manufacturer of VR equipment, acquired in 2014 by Facebook) researcher and professor at the University of Illinois, said on Facebook that he will join Huawei as the chief scientist of VR / AR / MR and will be responsible for Huawei's R & D in this field. In the evening of December 29, Huawei mobile phone product line president He Gang confirmed this news. In June of this year, He Gang in an interview said that Huawei plans to recruit the world's top technical talent.

      Steve Lavalle is a specialist in this area. Public information shows that in September 2012, he began working at Oculus VR until January 2015, he has been the main scientist of Oculus. In the meantime, he developed the IMU-based head tracking methodology for core software, and led the team of Perceptual Psychologists to provide the main solution for designing virtual reality calibration systems and user experiences comfortably. He is also one of the co-developers of the Oculus SDK and has helped Oculus develop two core technologies, including predictive tracking for reducing VR device stagnation, reducing stunning effects, and sensor calibration techniques.

      Lavalle said he and Huawei have same view on the VR / AR prospects, that we need to look at the overall perspective of this area of technology research and development, must be closely integrated hardware, software, human perception and neuroscience and other fields. He also mentioned that Huawei has a global business, and China is also emerging VR / AR market. Lavalle also announced to join Huawei's statement with his wearing a picture of Huawei workers.

      Chengdong Yu, Huawei CEO, also confirmed this news. Yu Chengdong said that he personally interviewed Lavalle, Huawei hopes to become one of the outstanding leaders of VR / AR field.
